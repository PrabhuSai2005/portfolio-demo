Decentralized Solar PV-Based Microgrid for Sustainable Rural Electrification

üåÄ Problem Statement

In many coastal regions of India, Primary Health Centers (PHCs) and rural clinics continue to face frequent and prolonged power outages, severely impacting their ability to operate essential medical equipment such as vaccine refrigerators, oxygen concentrators, and diagnostic tools. According to the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare (Rural Health Statistics Report 2023), nearly 70% of PHCs in coastal districts experience regular electricity disruptions. These clinics often depend on diesel generators, which not only increase operational costs but also contribute to air pollution and carbon emissions. The lack of reliable and affordable power supply compromises healthcare delivery, emergency response, and patient safety in underserved communities. This critical challenge calls for a sustainable, low-maintenance, and renewable power solution capable of ensuring 24/7 electricity without relying on fossil fuels.

üí° Innovative Solution

The proposed Smart VAWT Microgrid System harnesses coastal wind energy to provide a sustainable and uninterrupted power supply for rural health clinics. The system employs a Vertical Axis Wind Turbine (VAWT) designed for low-speed and variable wind conditions, converting wind kinetic energy into mechanical energy and subsequently into electrical energy through a generator. The generated DC power is stored in a battery bank and later converted to AC using a smart inverter and controller to power essential medical devices such as vaccine refrigerators and oxygen concentrators. An integrated IoT monitoring system enables real-time tracking of turbine performance, battery charge, and load distribution, ensuring predictive maintenance and high operational efficiency. This innovative setup significantly reduces diesel dependency, operational costs, and carbon emissions, providing a reliable and eco-friendly energy source for healthcare facilities in coastal regions.
