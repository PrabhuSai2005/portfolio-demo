Zero-Waste Biogas System for Sustainable Fuel and Fertilizer Production

üåÄ Problem Statement

Across India‚Äôs rural and semi-urban dairy farming regions, large amounts of cattle dung and agricultural waste are generated daily, often left to decompose in open areas. A single dairy cow produces nearly 10‚Äì15 kg of dung per day, meaning even small farms accumulate over a ton of organic waste daily. Improper waste disposal releases methane gas, a potent greenhouse pollutant, and creates sanitation and odor problems that affect nearby communities. Meanwhile, rising fertilizer prices and dependence on diesel for energy further strain rural livelihoods. According to the Ministry of New & Renewable Energy (MNRE 2024 Biogas Program) and the IPCC AR6 Climate Report, the potential of biogas from livestock waste remains vastly underutilized. This scenario underscores the urgent need for a zero-waste energy conversion system that transforms biomass into clean fuel (CBG) and organic fertilizer, creating economic value while reducing pollution and supporting India‚Äôs Waste-to-Wealth Mission.

üí° Innovative Solution

The proposed Zero-Waste Biogas Energy System transforms cattle dung into clean and renewable energy while promoting a circular rural economy. The system begins at the cattle farm, where dung is collected and mixed with water in a 1:1 ratio in the collection and mixing unit to form a slurry. This slurry is fed into an anaerobic digester, where microbial activity breaks down organic matter, producing biogas (methane-rich CBG) and digestate slurry. The biogas undergoes purification and compression to remove impurities like CO‚ÇÇ, H‚ÇÇS, and moisture, resulting in high-quality Compressed Biogas (CBG) that can be used for cooking, power generation, or vehicle fuel. Meanwhile, the leftover digestate is processed in a slurry composting unit to produce organic fertilizer, reducing chemical dependency and supporting sustainable agriculture. A Smart IoT Monitoring System tracks parameters such as pH, temperature, gas flow, and pressure, ensuring safe, efficient, and real-time operation. This integrated design enables zero waste generation, dual income opportunities from both CBG and fertilizer, and substantial reductions in methane emissions and air pollution, making it an ideal waste-to-energy solution for rural communities.

Source of Energy: The system utilizes dairy farm waste and agricultural residues as the main sources of biomass. Each dairy cow produces around 10‚Äì15 kg of dung daily, which becomes a valuable feedstock for sustainable energy generation in rural communities.

Conversion Process: The collected cattle dung is mixed with water in a 1:1 ratio and fed into an anaerobic digester, where microorganisms decompose organic matter in the absence of oxygen. The raw gas undergoes purification and compression to form Compressed Biogas (CBG), while the slurry is composted into organic fertilizer.

Output / Utilization: The purified CBG serves as a clean and renewable fuel for cooking, power generation, and vehicle applications. The composted slurry provides organic fertilizer to enrich soil health, reducing dependence on chemical fertilizers.

Real-world Relevance: This zero-waste model addresses the health and environmental impacts of open dumping and methane emissions.
